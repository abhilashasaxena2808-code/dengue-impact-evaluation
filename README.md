# dengue-impact-evaluation
Project using Causal Inference Techniques:                                                                                     Formulated an Observational Identification Strategy to isolate the causal impact of mosquito net usage on dengue risk for a sample of 1,752 households. 
Constructed and Evaluated a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) to explicitly map structural confounding pathways, successfully identifying the minimum sufficient adjustment set required to close backdoor paths and isolate the true Average Treatment Effect (ATE). 
Corrected for Selection Bias and Confounding across multiple observational frameworks by implementing and contrasting OLS regression adjustments, Nearest-Neighbour covariate matching, Propensity Score Matching (PSM), and Inverse Probability Weighting (IPW). 	
Designed a Quasi-Experimental 2SLS Instrumental Variables (IV) Design utilizing a multi-variable government policy threshold (household size and income limits) as an instrument for endogenous net adoption. Critically Validated the Exclusion Restriction by conditioning on the underlying assignment determinants (income and household size), resolving the threat of unconditional instrument invalidity to isolate a clean Local Average Treatment Effect (LATE). 
Analysed Policy Relevance and Heterogeneity by evaluating the divergence between OLS (ATE) and IV (LATE) estimates, characterizing the complier group, and testing for treatment effect heterogeneity to provide targeted public health recommendations. 
Synthesized Findings into a Rigorous Research Report, emphasizing the economic sensibility of the data, covariate balance metrics (Standardized Mean Differences), and the structural limitations of observational identification strategies.
